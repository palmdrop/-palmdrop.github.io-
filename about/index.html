About

It’s quite natural that new technologies and emerging media eventually are used as artistic tools. Digital art has been created for decades, there’s been a recent boom in AI art, and there are huge communities of generative artists working with code as their creative medium.

For many years now, I’ve been using generative/procedural techniques to learn more about programming and digital technologies, and simultaneously to realize my more aesthetic pursuits. I’ve experimented with well-known techniques such as domain warping and particle simulations, while also spending time with my own concepts and ideas.

My area of interest: the space where algorithms and digital art approximate something biological. Images that simultaneously look digital and organic have always been especially fascinating to me. There’s something strangely beautiful and eerily uncanny about them. I’d love to help map this space.

The Blog

This blog is dedicated to documenting some of the techniques and ideas I’ve been working on. Some may be original, some may not be. Regardless, the purpose is simply to describe my process, the experiments I’ve made, and possibly some of my ideas.

Anyone interested in my work specifically, or generative art generally, will probably find value in this blog. The most reliable way to get updates is to subscribe to my RSS feed.
